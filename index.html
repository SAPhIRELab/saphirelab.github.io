Objectives: Our goal is to advance trustworthy deployment of machine learning (ML) systems by ensuring:

  • Safety of users from deceptive or harmful model behavior
  • Accountability to verify claims made by practitioners
  • Privacy to minimize leakage of sensitive information
  • Impartiality to ensure equitable behavior across demographic groups
  • Robustness to prevent evasion of defenses by an adversary
  • Explainability to clarify the model components influencing specific behaviors
We view this from a systems security perspective covering (a) rigorous threat modeling for systematically identifying and mitigating risks, (b) holistic system analysis, and (c) enabling governance.

Research: We explore various concerns related to trustworthy deployment of ML systems covering

  • Assessing Risks: Define realistic threat models to evaluate risks to security, privacy, fairness, transparency
  • Designing Defenses: Formulate requirements (e.g., effective, efficient, robust) to design practical defenses
  • Holistic System Analysis: Study practical deployment concerns (e.g., interactions among defenses and risks)
  • Technical Governance: Design technical mechanisms for accountability and regulatory compliance
Our solutions take inspiration from diverse areas and topics: network and systems security, trusted computing, cryptography, and human-centered studies.
